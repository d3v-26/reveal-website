REVEAL: Multimodal Vision–Language Alignment of Retinal Morphometry and Clinical Risks for Incident AD and Dementia Prediction

Seowung Leem1, Lin Gu2, Chenyu You3,4, Kuang Gong1, Ruogu Fang1
1University of Florida, 2Tohoku University, 3Stony Brook University, 4Stony Brook University (CS)
MIDL 2026 submission PMLR (under review)
Paper (PDF) BibTeX

REVEAL aligns color fundus photographs with synthetic clinical risk narratives and improves early prediction of incident AD and dementia.

Abstract

The retina provides a unique, noninvasive window into Alzheimer’s disease and dementia, capturing early structural changes through morphometric features, while systemic and lifestyle risk factors reflect well-established contributors to AD and dementia susceptibility long before clinical symptom onset. However, current retinal analysis frameworks typically model imaging and risk factors separately, preventing them from capturing the joint multimodal patterns that are critical for early risk prediction. Moreover, existing methods rarely incorporate mechanisms to organize or align patients with similar retinal and clinical characteristics, limiting their ability to learn coherent cross-modal associations.

We introduce REVEAL (REtinal-risk Vision-language Early Alzheimer’s Learning) that aligns color fundus photographs with individualized disease-specific risk profiles for incident AD and dementia prediction on average 8 years before diagnosis. Because real-world risk factors are structured questionnaire data, we first translate them into clinically interpretable narratives compatible with pretrained vision-language models. We further propose a group-aware contrastive learning strategy that clusters patients with similar retinal morphometry and risk factors as positive pairs, strengthening multimodal alignment.

Method

Clinical report synthesis from structured risk factors

Structured questionnaire variables cannot be directly used as language input to a vision–language model. REVEAL converts each participant’s risk profile into a standardized, clinically styled narrative (CARE-guideline inspired) using an LLM, enabling seamless multimodal alignment.

Group-aware contrastive learning (GACL)

Instead of treating only same-subject image–text pairs as positives, GACL forms additional positives by grouping subjects who are similar in retinal morphometry and/or clinical report embedding space. This strengthens alignment and improves downstream incident AD/dementia prediction.

Results (from the manuscript)

Incident Alzheimer’s disease prediction

  • REVEAL (with GACL): AUROC 0.6439; Balanced Acc 0.6029; F1 0.2270; MCC 0.1562
  • RETFound + GatorTron: AUROC 0.6418
  • Baseline SVM (tabular + morphometry): AUROC 0.5721

Incident dementia prediction

  • REVEAL (with GACL): AUROC 0.6782; Balanced Acc 0.6294; F1 0.2599; MCC 0.1915
  • RETFound + GatorTron: AUROC 0.6596
  • Baseline SVM (tabular + morphometry): AUROC 0.5714

Values above are quoted from Tables 2–3 of the provided manuscript.
